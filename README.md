## Overview

Transformer-based language models excel at generating fluent text but exhibit a structural flaw: they collapse living human input into static probabilistic forms, causing users to feel progressively ejected from the creative process over extended interactions. We trace this to transformers operating in a single "probability octave" â€” generating variations within cached patterns rather than modulating to genuinely new semantic spaces.

This paper proposes **harmonic transformers**: an architecture where semantic relations are encoded as musical intervals (inspired by Steiner's emphasis on intervals as primary over tones, and Russell's octave structures), attention mechanisms detect harmonic resonance rather than statistical similarity, and outputs maintain unresolved dissonance (like a musical 7th chord) until human participation resolves or modulates the form.

**Important Framing:** This is a conceptual architecture proposal with testable hypotheses, not a finished implementation. The cross-domain patterns (quantum coherence, Bohm's ontology, Steiner's phenomenology) serve as conceptual motivation and theoretical grounding, not formal derivations. The architecture's validity depends on empirical testing, not on the strength of analogies.

## Empirical Testing Path

### Phase 0: Replication
Replicate Baek et al. (2025) HarMax results on GPT-2 to establish baseline interpretability.

### Phase 1: Hybrid Prototype
Implement resonance attention on HarMax-trained models. Test on analogy tasks.

### Phase 2: Synthetic Conversations
Multi-turn dialogues with scripted users. Measure D_octave and P_dissonance.

### Phase 3: Human Studies
A/B testing with real users over 20+ turn conversations. Quantitative metrics + qualitative interviews.

## Falsifiability Conditions

The hypothesis **fails** if:

1. Harmonic transformers show **lower** octave distance than standard transformers (indicating less novelty)
2. Dissonance persistence drops to zero despite architectural constraints
3. User sovereignty metrics show no significant difference or favor standard transformers
